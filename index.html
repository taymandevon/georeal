About Snapchat

Snapchat is a free social media app that enables quick, honest communication. The premise of the app is that instead of the highly edited pictures and videos of other social media; Snapchat is an unabridged glimpse at the person behind the username. Snapchat presents three main options for its users:

Individual Pictures

Users can take a picture using the front or rear facing camera on their smartphone, add a caption, some artwork with virtual brushes of any color, a virtual mask, emojis, or a Geofilter. Users can send these images to any number of people. The image lasts for a time between 1 and 10 seconds, as chosen by the sender, then disappears forever.

Individual Videos

These work the same way as pictures do, but it is a 1 to 10 second video, rather than a picture.

Stories

The Story is a very powerful tool for mass communication. A user can post an image or a video to their story, with the same customization tools, but rather than being sent to individuals, every single user connected to the poster will see the Story.

Geofilters

A Geofilter is a precisely designed image overlay that denotes the location in which a Snap is being taken. The filter is specific to only one location, which is decided by the owner of the filter. When any Snapchat user takes a picture or video in the location set by the owner of the filter, they are given the option to use the filter on their Snap.

Why do people use Geofilters?

A Geofilter does many things to an otherwise normal Snap. Firstly, it adds visual interest to the picture or video. Additonally, it tels whoever views the Snap where it was taken. Lastly, and most importantly, it adds production value to any Snap. To use a certain filter, it means you had to be in a certain location. For example, a Pitsford Village filter tells the audience that the picture was taken in the Pittsford Village, which means the creator had to be out of the house and in the village to share whatever they did.
